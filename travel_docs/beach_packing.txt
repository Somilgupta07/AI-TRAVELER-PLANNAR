Beach and Coastal Destination Packing Guide

Essential Clothing for Beach Trips:
- Lightweight cotton or linen clothing: Breathes well in humid coastal weather.
- Swimwear/swimsuit: Pack 2 so one can dry while wearing the other.
- Sarong or pareo: Multi-purpose beach cover-up, towel, and shawl in one.
- Flip-flops or waterproof sandals: Easy to wear on the beach and in the water.
- Light casual footwear for evenings: Simple sneakers or flat sandals.
- Shorts (2-3 pairs): Quick-dry material preferred.
- Comfortable tops and t-shirts (4-5): Light colors to reflect heat.
- One light jacket or cardigan: Evenings can be surprisingly cool, and restaurants with AC are cold.
- Sun hat with wide brim: Essential for beach sun protection.
- UV-protective sunglasses: Quality lenses that block UVA and UVB.

For Goa Specifically:
- Smart-casual outfit for beach clubs: Many places have dress codes in the evening.
- Light dress or kurta for temple/church visits: Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Toiletries and Sun Care:
- High SPF sunscreen (SPF 50+, water-resistant): Most important item. Reapply every 2 hours.
- After-sun lotion or aloe vera gel: Soothes sunburn.
- Insect repellent: Mosquitoes are common near coastal areas, especially at dawn and dusk.
- Lip balm with SPF: Lips get chapped and sunburned easily.
- After-bite cream or antihistamine: For bug bites and itching.
- Waterproof mascara and light makeup: Regular makeup melts in beach heat.
- Dry shampoo: Hair gets greasy quickly in humid conditions.
- Mild shampoo and conditioner: Salt water strips hair moisture.

Beach Essentials:
- Quick-dry microfiber towel: Dries fast, takes up less space than regular towels.
- Beach bag (water-resistant tote): For carrying essentials to the beach.
- Waterproof phone pouch: Protect your phone from water and sand.
- Ziplock bags: Keep sand and water away from electronics and valuables.
- Underwater camera or GoPro: For water activities like snorkeling and scuba.
- Reusable water bottle: Stay hydrated. Avoid buying single-use plastic bottles.

Health and Medicines:
- Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS): For heat exhaustion and dehydration.
- Antidiarrheal tablets (Loperamide): Seafood can sometimes cause stomach issues.
- Antibiotic tablets (consult doctor): For bacterial infections from water or food.
- Paracetamol: For heat headaches and fever.
- Band-aids and antiseptic: For cuts from coral or rocks.

Watersport Preparation:
- Rash guard: Protects from sunburn during extended water activities.
- Aqua shoes/water shoes: For rocky beaches and water entry/exit.
- Snorkeling gear (mask and fins): If you plan to snorkel, renting on-site is also an option.
- Life jacket: Always wear one for water sports if provided.

Evening and Nightlife:
- One or two smart-casual outfits: For beach clubs and restaurant dinners.
- Light scarf or stole: For evenings near the water.
- Comfortable party footwear: That you don't mind getting a little dirty.

Documents:
- Government ID: Aadhar, Passport, or Driver's License.
- Hotel booking confirmation.
- Travel insurance documents.
- Emergency contact numbers written down on paper.

Tips for Beach Destinations:
- Do not take valuables to the beach. Leave jewelry, laptop, and extra cash at the hotel.
- Most beach shacks store water at room temperature. Buy cold water from shops.
- Avoid swimming in the sea during monsoon. The undertow is very dangerous.
- Be respectful of local fishing communities on beaches.
