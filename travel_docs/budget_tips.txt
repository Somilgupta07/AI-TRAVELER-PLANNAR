Budget Travel Tips for India

General Budget Travel Strategy:
- Traveling in India can be done comfortably on Rs 1500-2500 per day per person including accommodation, food, and local transport.
- Budget backpackers can manage on Rs 800-1200/day by staying in hostels, eating at local dhabas, and using public transport.
- Mid-range travelers typically spend Rs 3000-6000/day for comfortable hotels and occasional restaurants.
- Luxury travel starts at Rs 8000-15000/day for premium hotels and experiences.

Accommodation Cost Ranges:
- Dormitory hostels: Rs 300-600/bed/night in most cities.
- Budget guesthouses: Rs 500-1200/night for a private room.
- Mid-range hotels (3-star): Rs 1500-3500/night.
- Luxury hotels (4-5 star): Rs 5000-25000/night.
- Camps and homestays: Rs 800-2500/night with meals.

Food Cost Ranges:
- Local dhaba or street food meal: Rs 50-150 per person.
- Budget restaurant meal: Rs 150-300 per person.
- Mid-range restaurant meal: Rs 400-800 per person.
- Fine dining: Rs 1200-3000+ per person.
- 1 liter bottled water: Rs 20-30.
- Tea/chai from street stall: Rs 10-20.

Transport Cost Ranges:
- Local auto-rickshaw (per km): Rs 10-20.
- Cab (Ola/Uber per km): Rs 8-15 depending on city.
- State government bus (long distance): Rs 0.4-0.8 per km.
- Volvo overnight bus (Delhi to Manali ~500 km): Rs 800-1500.
- Train (sleeper class per 100 km): Rs 70-120.
- Train (AC 3-tier per 100 km): Rs 200-350.
- Domestic flight: Rs 2000-7000 depending on route and booking time.

How to Save Money on Travel:
1. Book trains at least 30-60 days in advance on IRCTC for lower fares.
2. Use government MSRTC/HRTC/KSRTC buses for inter-city travel instead of private buses.
3. Eat where locals eat: dhabas, thalis, and street stalls offer the best value.
4. Stay in hostels to save on accommodation and meet fellow travelers.
5. Travel during shoulder season (avoid peak holidays like Diwali, Christmas) for 30-50% cheaper rates.
6. Use Google Maps and local buses instead of tourist taxis.
7. Negotiate prices at handicraft shops, but be respectful. Starting at 40-50% of asking price is common.
8. Buy water in large 1L bottles instead of 500ml bottles.
9. Carry a reusable water bottle and refill at hotels.
10. Book accommodations 2-3 weeks in advance for popular destinations to get better rates.

Budget for Popular Destinations (per person, per day):
- Delhi: Rs 1500-3000 (budget to mid-range)
- Goa (peak season): Rs 2500-5000
- Manali: Rs 1500-3000
- Ladakh: Rs 2500-4500 (higher due to remoteness)
- Jaipur: Rs 1500-2500
- Rishikesh: Rs 1000-2000
- Varanasi: Rs 1000-1800

Activities Cost:
- Paragliding in Bir Billing/Solang: Rs 1500-2500.
- River rafting (Rishikesh, Manali): Rs 600-1500.
- Rohtang Pass permit (Manali): Rs 600 per vehicle + Rs 100 environment fee.
- National park entry fees: Rs 200-500 per person.
- Fort/monument entry (ASI sites): Rs 15-50 (Indian), Rs 250-600 (foreign).
- Cooking class: Rs 500-1500.
- Bike rental (Ladakh): Rs 1500-3500/day.
