Jaipur Travel Guide

Jaipur, known as the "Pink City", is the capital of Rajasthan, India. It is part of the famous Golden Triangle tourist circuit along with Delhi and Agra. The city is famous for its stunning Rajput-era architecture, vibrant culture, royal palaces, and colorful bazaars.

Best Time to Visit:
- October to March is ideal with cool and pleasant weather (8-25°C).
- April to June is very hot (up to 45°C), not recommended.
- July to September is monsoon season, less crowded and greenery adds beauty.
- January hosts the famous Jaipur Literature Festival (JLF), one of the world's largest literary festivals.

Top Attractions:
- Amber Fort (Amer Fort): A magnificent hilltop fort with stunning Rajput and Mughal architecture. Elephant rides available.
- Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds): Iconic 5-story honeycomb facade, built so royal ladies could watch street festivities.
- City Palace: A complex of palaces, courtyards, and museums still partially occupied by the royal family.
- Jantar Mantar: UNESCO-listed astronomical observatory with massive stone instruments, built in 1734.
- Nahargarh Fort: Located on the Aravalli Hills, great sunset views over the city.
- Jal Mahal: Beautiful palace appearing to float in Man Sagar Lake, best seen at dusk.
- Birla Mandir: A stunning white marble temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu.

Shopping:
- Johari Bazaar: Famous for gemstones, jewelry, and lac bangles.
- Bapu Bazaar: Best for traditional Rajasthani textiles, mojris (leather shoes), and handicrafts.
- Tripolia Bazaar: For lac jewelry and metalwork.
- MI Road: Mix of local and branded shops.

Local Food:
- Dal Baati Churma: Rajasthan's most iconic dish. Hard wheat rolls served with spicy lentil soup and sweet churma.
- Laal Maas: Fiery mutton curry in a red chili base, a true Rajasthani delicacy.
- Pyaaz Kachori: Deep-fried pastry stuffed with spiced onion filling, best eaten fresh.
- Ghewar: Disc-shaped sweet made from flour and dipped in sugar syrup, especially popular during festivals.
- Mirchi Bada: Large green chili dipped in spiced potato filling and deep-fried.
- Lassi at Lassiwala (MI Road): Famous thick Jaipur lassi in earthen pots.

Cultural Experiences:
- Chokhi Dhani: Rajasthani village resort with folk dances, camel rides, and traditional dinner.
- Block printing workshops: Try traditional Bagru and Sanganer block printing.
- Puppet shows: Traditional Rajasthani string puppet (Kathputli) performances.

Getting There:
- By Air: Jaipur International Airport has direct flights from Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore.
- By Train: Jaipur Junction is very well connected. Pink City Express from Delhi takes about 5 hours.
- By Road: 5-6 hours from Delhi via NH48 Expressway. Plenty of Volvo buses available.

Accommodation:
- Budget: Hostels near Badi Chaupar (Rs 400-800/night)
- Heritage hotels: Alsisar Haveli, Samode Haveli (Rs 3000-8000/night)
- Luxury: Rambagh Palace, Taj Jai Mahal Palace (Rs 15,000+/night)
