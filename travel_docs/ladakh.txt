Ladakh Travel Guide

Ladakh is a union territory in northern India, situated in the western Himalayas between the Karakoram range and the Himalayas. Known as "Land of High Passes", it is one of the world's most spectacular regions with barren landscapes, Buddhist monasteries, and high-altitude lakes.

Best Time to Visit:
- June to September is the main travel season. Roads are open and weather is pleasant (10-25°C during day).
- May is good if you want to beat crowds.
- October offers clear skies and fewer tourists, but it gets cold.
- November to April: Most roads close due to heavy snowfall. Only accessible by flight.
- Chadar Trek (frozen Zanskar River trek) happens in January-February.

Top Attractions:
- Pangong Tso Lake: Famous high-altitude lake at 4,350m stretching into China. Blue color changes with the sun. Featured in "3 Idiots" movie.
- Nubra Valley: Sand dunes with Bactrian camels in a mountain desert, accessible via Khardung La pass.
- Khardung La Pass: One of the world's highest motorable roads at 5,359 meters.
- Leh Palace: 17th-century palace overlooking Leh town, built like Potala Palace in Tibet.
- Hemis Monastery: Largest and wealthiest monastery in Ladakh. Hosts the famous Hemis Festival in June/July.
- Thiksey Monastery: Stunning 12-story monastery complex, resembling the Potala Palace.
- Magnetic Hill: Optical illusion that makes vehicles appear to roll uphill.
- Zanskar Valley: Remote valley with ancient monasteries and the legendary Chadar Trek.

Altitude Sickness:
- Leh is at 3,524 meters. Altitude sickness is common.
- Spend the first day in Leh resting without doing any activities.
- Avoid alcohol and smoking for the first two days.
- Drink plenty of water (3-4 liters/day).
- Carry Diamox (acetazolamide) tablets as preventive medicine. Consult a doctor before use.
- Symptoms: Headache, nausea, fatigue, breathlessness. If severe, descend immediately.

Activities:
- River rafting on the Zanskar and Indus rivers.
- Mountain biking on Leh-Manali Highway.
- Trekking: Markha Valley Trek, Stok Kangri Trek, Chadar Trek.
- Camel safari in Hunder (Nubra Valley).
- Motorbiking: Leh to Manali and Leh to Srinagar are legendary biker routes.
- Stargazing: Ladakh has minimal light pollution, perfect for astrophotography.

Local Food:
- Thukpa: Tibetan noodle soup with vegetables or meat, warming and hearty.
- Momos: Steamed or fried dumplings filled with vegetables or yak meat.
- Tsampa: Roasted barley flour mixed with butter tea, a staple Ladakhi food.
- Butter Tea (Gur Gur Chai): Salty tea made with yak butter, an acquired taste.
- Skyu: Traditional stew with root vegetables and wheat flour pieces.
- Chhang: Local barley beer, refreshing in summer.

Getting There:
- By Air: Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport in Leh has flights from Delhi, Mumbai, Srinagar, and Jammu.
- By Road: Manali-Leh Highway (opens June) — 490 km, 2 days drive via Keylong.
- Srinagar-Leh Highway (opens May) — 434 km, 1-2 days drive.
- Road journeys are highly scenic and popular with adventure travelers.

Permits Required:
- Inner Line Permit (ILP) required for protected areas: Pangong Tso, Nubra Valley, Tso Moriri.
- Available online or at DC Office in Leh. Bring ID proof.
- Foreign nationals need a Protected Area Permit (PAP).

Accommodation:
- Budget: Guesthouses in Leh town (Rs 600-1500/night)
- Mid-range: Camps at Pangong Lake, Nubra (Rs 2500-5000 including meals)
- Luxury: The Grand Dragon Ladakh, Nimmu House (Rs 8000+/night)
