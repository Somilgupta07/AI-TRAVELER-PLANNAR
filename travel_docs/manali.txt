Manali Travel Guide

Manali is a high-altitude Himalayan resort town in Himachal Pradesh, India, set in the Beas River Valley. It is a popular destination for honeymooners, adventure seekers, and nature lovers.

Best Time to Visit:
- October to June is ideal for most travelers.
- December to February is perfect for snow lovers and skiing.
- July to September brings heavy monsoon rains and road closures.
- May and June are peak summer months with pleasant weather around 10-25°C.

Top Attractions:
- Rohtang Pass: A high mountain pass at 3,978 meters, offering stunning snow views and adventure activities. Requires a permit.
- Solang Valley: Famous for skiing in winter and paragliding, zorbing, and horse riding in summer.
- Hadimba Temple: A beautiful wooden temple surrounded by cedar forest, dedicated to Goddess Hadimba.
- Old Manali: A charming village with cafes, apple orchards, and backpacker culture.
- Beas Kund Trek: A moderate trek to the source of the Beas River, suitable for beginners.
- Naggar Castle: A medieval castle overlooking the Kullu valley, now converted into a heritage hotel.
- Vashisht Village: Known for hot spring baths and the Vashisht temple.

Local Food:
- Siddu: A local wheat bread stuffed with poppy seeds, walnut, or spinach filling.
- Trout Fish: Freshly caught from Beas River, cooked with local spices.
- Dham: Traditional festive meal served on banana leaves with rice, lentils, and seasonal vegetables.
- Aktori: A pancake made from buckwheat flour, typically eaten in winters.
- Babru: A deep-fried bread similar to kachori, filled with black lentils.
- Chha Gosht: Marinated lamb cooked in yogurt and spices.
- Apple products: Manali is famous for apples. Try fresh juice, cider, and apple jam.

Activities:
- Trekking routes to Beas Kund, Chandrakhani Pass, and Pin Parvati Pass.
- River rafting on the Beas River between Pirdi and Jhiri.
- Camping under stars in Solang Valley and Sissu.
- Mountain biking on Rohtang Pass trails.
- Paragliding in Solang Valley and Dobhi.

Getting There:
- Nearest airport: Bhuntar Airport (Kullu-Manali Airport), about 50 km away.
- From Delhi: Volvo overnight bus takes about 12-14 hours. Highly recommended.
- From Chandigarh: 7-8 hours by road.
- Nearest railway station: Joginder Nagar (108 km) or Chandigarh (310 km).

Accommodation:
- Budget: Old Manali hostels and guesthouses (Rs 400-800/night)
- Mid-range: Hotels near Mall Road (Rs 1500-3000/night)
- Luxury: Span Resort, Johnson's Lodge (Rs 5000+/night)

Travel Tips:
- Carry warm clothes even in summer as evenings get cold.
- Book Rohtang Pass permits online via the official government portal.
- ATMs are available on Mall Road but carry cash for remote areas.
- Altitude can cause mild headaches. Stay hydrated and rest on first day.
- Do not litter in the mountains. Manali has eco-sensitive zones.
