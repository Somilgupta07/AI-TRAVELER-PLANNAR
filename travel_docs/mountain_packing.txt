Mountain and Hill Station Packing Guide

Essential Clothing for Mountain Trips:
- Thermal innerwear (top and bottom): Non-negotiable for cold nights. Carry 2-3 sets.
- Fleece jacket or mid-layer: Acts as insulation. Easy to layer.
- Windproof and waterproof outer jacket: Essential for sudden weather changes.
- Heavy winter jacket or down jacket: Needed for high altitudes like Ladakh, Spiti, Rohtang Pass.
- Trekking trousers (quick-dry material): 2 pairs. Avoid jeans as they are heavy and take long to dry.
- Comfortable walking shoes or trekking boots: Waterproof with ankle support.
- Woolen socks (thick): Carry 4-5 pairs. Feet get cold quickly.
- Warm gloves: Waterproof preferred. Fleece gloves work for lower altitudes.
- Woolen cap or beanie: A large amount of body heat is lost through the head.
- Balaclava: For very cold conditions (Ladakh, Spiti in shoulder months).
- Neck gaiter or scarf: Protects from wind chill.
- UV-protective sunglasses: High altitude UV radiation is intense. Get CE-rated glasses.
- Sun hat or cap: For daytime activities.
- Light cotton t-shirts: 2-3 for warmer days. Layers are key.

Toiletries and Health:
- High SPF sunscreen (SPF 50+): UV is much stronger at altitude. Reapply every 2 hours.
- Lip balm with SPF: Lips crack easily in cold and dry mountain air.
- Moisturizer: Mountain air is dry. Use a good moisturizer.
- Blister patches: If doing long walks or treks.
- Hand sanitizer and wet wipes: Basic hygiene in remote areas.
- Personal medication: Any regular prescriptions plus common remedies.

Medicines to Carry for Mountains:
- Diamox (Acetazolamide): Altitude sickness prevention. Consult a doctor before use.
- Paracetamol or Ibuprofen: For headaches (common at altitude).
- ORS packets: Oral Rehydration Salts for staying hydrated.
- Antacid tablets: Mountain food can be heavy.
- Cough drops and throat lozenges: Cold, dry air irritates the throat.
- Band-aids and antiseptic cream: For minor cuts and scrapes.

Gear and Accessories:
- Daypack (20-30 liters): For carrying essentials during day trips.
- Reusable water bottle or hydration bladder (at least 1 liter capacity): Stay hydrated.
- Water purification tablets or LifeStraw filter: For remote areas without clean water.
- Trekking poles: Reduces knee strain on descents. Very helpful for long hikes.
- Headlamp with extra batteries: Power cuts are common, and trekking after dark needs light.
- Power bank (10,000 mAh+): Charging outlets are scarce in remote areas.
- Universal travel adapter.
- Dry bags or waterproof covers for electronics and important documents.

Documents and Money:
- Government-issued photo ID (Aadhar, Passport, or Voter ID): Required at checkpoints.
- Inner Line Permits (for Ladakh restricted areas): Download or get in Leh.
- Travel insurance documents.
- Cash in small denominations: ATMs are rare in remote mountain areas. Carry at least Rs 3000-5000 cash.
- Hotel/camp booking confirmations printed or downloaded offline.

Tech and Electronics:
- Phone with offline maps downloaded (Google Maps, Maps.me).
- Extra phone charging cable.
- Camera with extra memory card: Mountain landscapes are photograph-worthy.
- Earphones.

What NOT to Carry:
- Heavy woolen sweaters (take up too much bag space, use thermal layers instead).
- Too many clothes (pack light, do laundry).
- High heels or formal shoes.
- Too many gadgets.
- Lots of cash in one place (divide it).
