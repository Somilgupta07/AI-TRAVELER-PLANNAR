Travel Safety Tips for India

General Safety:
- Share your travel itinerary with a trusted friend or family member before leaving.
- Save emergency numbers: Police (100), Ambulance (108), Women's Helpline (1091), Tourist Police (1800111363).
- Keep digital copies of all documents (passport, ID, hotel bookings) on Google Drive or email.
- Carry photocopies of important documents separately from originals.
- Avoid displaying expensive jewelry, cameras, or gadgets in crowded areas.
- Use hotel safes for passports, extra cash, and valuable items.

Transport Safety:
- Always use app-based cabs (Ola, Uber, Rapido) over street hailing for transparency.
- Share your Ola/Uber ride details with a contact before getting in.
- Avoid empty trains or metro coaches late at night. Stay in general populated areas.
- Women should sit in women-only compartments in metro and trains for added safety.
- In hill stations, only hire government-registered vehicles for mountain routes.
- Do not travel on mountain roads at night. Visibility is poor and roads are dangerous.
- Always wear helmets if riding motorbikes.

Accommodation Safety:
- Check hotel reviews on Google/TripAdvisor before booking.
- Always lock your room door and use the chain lock.
- Do not open the door to strangers without verifying their identity with the hotel.
- In hostels, use a luggage lock for your bag and the hostel locker.
- Keep emergency hotel exit routes in mind.

Food and Water Safety:
- Drink bottled or filtered water. Avoid tap water everywhere in India.
- Be cautious with street food from unhygienic-looking stalls.
- In coastal areas, eat at popular, well-reviewed seafood restaurants to avoid stomach infections.
- Avoid raw salads at street stalls (made with potentially unwashed vegetables).
- Carry ORS packets and basic stomach medicines.
- Inform the server if you have food allergies.

Health Precautions:
- Carry a basic first aid kit with band-aids, antiseptic, paracetamol, and common medicines.
- Stay hydrated especially in hot and high-altitude destinations.
- Apply mosquito repellent in forest areas, hill stations, and coastal areas at dawn/dusk.
- Get travel vaccinations if going to remote areas (Typhoid, Hepatitis A, Tetanus).
- In case of altitude sickness, descend immediately and seek medical help.

Safety for Women Travelers:
- Dress modestly in religious sites and conservative towns to avoid unwanted attention.
- Trust your instincts. If a situation feels unsafe, leave immediately.
- Carry a personal alarm or pepper spray (check local laws first).
- Avoid walking alone late at night in unfamiliar areas.
- Use women-only hostels or reputed accommodations verified by other women travelers.
- Research destinations on solo female travel forums before visiting.
- Keep a fully charged phone with emergency contacts saved.

Digital and Financial Safety:
- Use a VPN when connecting to public WiFi.
- Do not use public charging stations (juice jacking risk). Carry your own power bank.
- Inform your bank before traveling to avoid your card being blocked.
- Carry a backup debit/credit card stored separately.
- Use ATMs inside banks or shopping malls rather than standalone roadside ATMs.
- Do not hand your card to anyone. Always insert or tap it yourself.

Wildlife and Nature Safety:
- Do not feed or approach wild animals.
- Follow park rangers' instructions in national parks and wildlife reserves.
- Do not swim in lakes or rivers unless it is clearly marked as safe.
- Check for jellyfish warnings before swimming in the sea.
- During monsoon, avoid trekking near rivers and waterfalls due to flash flood risk.

Scam Awareness:
- Be wary of "friendly strangers" who approach you near tourist spots offering unsolicited help.
- Verify taxi/auto meter is running, or agree on price before getting in.
- Gem scams are common in tourist cities like Jaipur and Agra. Do not buy gems for "export profit".
- Check your bill at restaurants before paying. Extra items are sometimes added.
- Do not accept "free" garlands, tilaks, or gifts near temples. They will demand money.
