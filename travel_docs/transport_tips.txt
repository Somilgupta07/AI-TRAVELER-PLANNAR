Transport and Getting Around India - Tips

Trains in India:
- Indian Railways is one of the world's largest railway networks. Book on IRCTC (irctc.co.in) website or app.
- Train classes: General > Sleeper (SL) > AC 3-tier (3A) > AC 2-tier (2A) > First Class AC (1A).
- For budget travel, Sleeper class is affordable and an authentic experience.
- For overnight long-distance trips, AC 3-tier is the best value comfort option.
- Book at least 30-60 days in advance for popular routes, especially during holidays.
- Use the "Tatkal" quota if booking at the last minute (Rs 100-400 extra surcharge).
- Check PNR status using the IRCTC app or SMS "PNR XXXXXXXXXX" to 139.
- Arrive at the station 20 minutes before departure. Platform numbers can change.

Buses in India:
- State government buses (KSRTC, MSRTC, HRTC, RSRTC) are safe and affordable.
- Volvo sleeper buses are ideal for overnight city-to-city travel. Book on RedBus app.
- Prices: Non-AC bus Rs 0.4-0.7/km, AC Volvo Rs 1-2/km.
- For hill stations, state HRTC buses are cheap but take longer than private buses.

Flights in India:
- Book flights 4-8 weeks in advance for cheapest fares. Prices spike during holidays.
- Use Skyscanner, Google Flights, or MakeMyTrip to compare fares.
- Budget airlines: IndiGo, SpiceJet, Air India Express for cheapest options.
- Full service: Air India, Vistara for better service and free meals.
- Carry-on limit: typically 7-8 kg. Pay extra for checked baggage if needed.
- Web check-in opens 24-48 hours before flight.
- Reach airport 2 hours before domestic departure, 3 hours for international.

Metro and City Transport:
- Metro systems: Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Chennai, Kolkata, Jaipur, Kochi.
- Buy a metro card for easier travel (deposit + recharge). Much faster than token queues.
- Fares: Rs 10-60 typically within a city.
- Auto-rickshaws: Negotiate price first, or insist on meter. Very useful for short distances.
- In major cities, always prefer app-based autos (Namma Yatri, Rapido Auto) to avoid overcharging.

App-Based Cabs and Bikes:
- Ola and Uber operate in most major cities and hill stations.
- Rapido Bike is great for solo travelers in city traffic and cheaper than autos.
- Always verify driver name and vehicle number before getting in.
- Share your ride with emergency contacts using the in-app share feature.

Renting Vehicles:
- Renting a scooty/motorbike is popular in Goa (Rs 300-600/day) and Manali (Rs 500-800/day).
- Carry your driving license (Indian DL or International DL for foreign nationals).
- Always carry helmet. Traffic police are strict about helmets in tourist areas.
- Check bike brakes and tyres before renting.
- For Ladakh, rent a Royal Enfield (Rs 1500-3500/day) from reputed shops in Leh.
- Self-drive cars can be rented from Zoomcar and Myles in major cities.

Taxis and Cabs:
- Prepaid taxis from airports are safe and convenient. Counters are just outside arrivals.
- Outside prepaid counters, negotiate price firmly before getting in any cab.
- Avoid taxis that offer unsolicited help at tourist spots.

Last-Mile Connectivity in Remote Areas:
- In remote Himalayan areas (Spiti, Zanskar, parts of Ladakh), shared jeeps are the main transport.
- Shared jeeps run on fixed routes and are much cheaper than private cabs.
- Ask locals or your guesthouse about shared jeep timings and pick-up points.
- Local buses in remote areas run once daily, usually early morning.

Ride-sharing for Long Routes:
- BlaBlaCar app: Find carpools for long intercity routes. Safe and affordable.
- Facebook groups for travelers going to Manali, Leh, Spiti etc. often have carpool posts.

Driving Tips:
- Mountain roads have blind corners. Always honk before turning.
- Avoid overtaking on mountain roads.
- Roads in Ladakh and Spiti are unpaved. SUV or high-ground-clearance vehicles recommended.
- Keep fuel tank full in remote areas. Petrol pumps are far apart.
